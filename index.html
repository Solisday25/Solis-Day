About Solis Day Design Studio

Studio

Founded on a family legacy passed from father to daughters, Solis Day is now a sister-run landscape design studio led by licensed Landscape Designer Lacie Peralta.

§ Our Philosophy

Thoughtful water-wise outdoor spaces tailored to the way people live.

01

Health

We design with the land's living systems in mind — improving soil structure, supporting pollinators, and selecting plants that strengthen the local ecology rather than strain it. A healthy garden gives back as much as it receives.

02

Beauty

Beauty in a landscape is earned through proportion, material honesty, and the unhurried accumulation of patina. We seek the kind of quiet elegance that deepens with each season rather than dazzles on the day it opens.

03

Social

Outdoor spaces are where people gather, linger, and reconnect. Every design considers how a garden invites use — shaded spots for conversation, open ground for children, and thresholds that welcome neighbours and guests alike.

04

Sustainability

We work with climate, not against it. Water-wise planting, permeable surfaces, and materials sourced close to the site reduce ongoing impact. Our goal is a garden that thrives on what rain and sun provide, long after we have handed over the key.

05

Function

A beautiful space that does not work for its owners is unfinished. We map movement, storage, drainage, and seasonal use from the very first sketch, so the finished landscape feels effortless to inhabit and straightforward to maintain.

§ Process

From the first conversation to the final planting, we guide you through a seamless design journey that transforms your outdoor space into a place that feels uniquely yours.

01 / 06

Consultation

Understanding your vision, lifestyle, and the unique characteristics of your property.

  • Client Goals — Define how you want to use the space, your priorities, and the experience you want the landscape to create.
  • Site Assessment — Analyze the existing conditions, including sunlight, soil, drainage, climate, and existing features.
  • Budget — Establish an investment range to guide realistic design decisions and material selections.
  • Style Preferences — Identify your preferred aesthetic, such as Mediterranean, modern, naturalistic, or traditional.
02 / 06

Concept Design

Transforming ideas into an initial vision for the overall landscape experience.

  • Layout Options — Explore different spatial arrangements to optimize flow, function, and visual balance.
  • Space Planning — Organize areas for gathering, relaxation, movement, gardens, and outdoor activities.
  • Planting Concept — Develop a preliminary plant strategy based on beauty, climate, maintenance, and seasonal interest.
  • Material Palette — Select the overall character of materials, textures, colors, and finishes for the landscape.
03 / 06

Design Development

Refining the concept into a detailed and buildable landscape design.

  • Hardscape Design — Design pathways, patios, walls, structures, and outdoor living features that shape the space.
  • Plant Selection — Choose specific plants that support the design vision, biodiversity, and long-term health.
  • Lighting — Create a lighting plan that enhances beauty, safety, and nighttime atmosphere.
  • Irrigation — Develop an efficient watering strategy tailored to plant needs and water conservation.
  • Drainage — Address water movement and grading to protect the landscape and property.
04 / 06

Construction Documents

Creating the detailed plans needed to accurately bring the design to life.

  • Master Plan — Provide a complete overview showing the location and relationship of all landscape elements.
  • Planting Plan — Document plant species, quantities, placement, and spacing for installation.
  • Materials & Specifications — Define the exact products, finishes, and construction requirements.
  • Dimensions & Details — Provide technical drawings and measurements to ensure accurate execution.
05 / 06

Installation

Bringing the approved design from drawings into a finished outdoor environment.

  • Site Preparation — Prepare the land through grading, soil improvements, and removal of unwanted elements.
  • Hardscape Construction — Build patios, pathways, walls, structures, and other permanent features.
  • Planting — Install trees, shrubs, grasses, and flowers according to the design plan.
  • Irrigation & Lighting — Install systems that support plant health and enhance the outdoor experience.
  • Final Touches — Add finishing elements that complete the landscape and refine the details.
06 / 06

Aftercare

Ensuring the landscape thrives and continues to improve over time.

  • Maintenance Guide — Provide care instructions for plants, materials, and landscape systems.
  • Plant Establishment — Support healthy growth during the critical early stages after installation.
  • Seasonal Adjustments — Adapt maintenance practices as plants mature and seasons change.
  • Long-Term Care — Maintain the beauty, function, and sustainability of the landscape for years to come.
